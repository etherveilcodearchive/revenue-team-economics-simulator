Planning workspace

Inputs first. Economics second. Decision last.

The deterministic simulator stays focused on facts, numbers, relationships, charts, and calculated outputs. Interpretation lives in the analyst layer.

1

Context

Define the decision.

Anchor the model around the company, planning period, and strategic question. This prevents the output from becoming a generic calculator result.

2

Revenue motions

Choose what belongs in the plan.

Select only the acquisition paths that are part of this decision. Inactive motions stay out of the economics and final artifact.

3

Assumptions

Configure the model.

Enter motion-level cost, volume, and conversion assumptions. This area is for inputs, not recommendations.

4

Review the relationships before making a decision: profit by motion, CAC by motion, funnel volume, and the audit table.
